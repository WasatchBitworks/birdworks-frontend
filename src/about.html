About BirdNET-Pi

Real-time bird identification using artificial intelligence

What is BirdNET?

BirdNET is a research platform that uses deep learning and artificial intelligence to identify bird species by their vocalizations. Developed jointly by the Cornell Lab of Ornithology and Chemnitz University of Technology, BirdNET can recognize over 6,000 bird species from around the world.

The system analyzes audio recordings and compares them against a massive database of bird calls and songs, providing identification with a confidence score. This technology makes it possible to monitor bird populations continuously and non-invasively.

How Our System Works

1

Continuous Audio Monitoring

A microphone captures ambient audio from our location on the Wasatch Front, Utah. The system records 15-second audio segments continuously throughout the day and night.

2

AI Analysis

Each audio segment is analyzed by the BirdNET neural network running on a Raspberry Pi 5. The AI identifies potential bird calls and songs, comparing them against its trained model of thousands of species.

3

Confidence Scoring

When a bird is detected, the system assigns a confidence score (0-100%) indicating how certain the AI is about the identification. Higher scores generally indicate more reliable detections.

4

Data Storage & Display

Detection data is synced to our database every 5 minutes. This website displays the latest detections, species statistics, and historical trends.

Technical Details

Hardware

  • • Raspberry Pi 5
  • • USB Microphone
  • • Debian 12 (Bookworm)

Software

  • • BirdNET-Pi (Nachtzuster fork)
  • • BirdNET 6K Global Model v2.4
  • • PostgreSQL Database

Location

  • • Wasatch Front, Utah
  • • ~40.56°N, 111.85°W
  • • Elevation: ~4,500 ft

Detection Settings

  • • Confidence threshold: 70%
  • • Sensitivity: 1.25
  • • Audio format: FLAC

Note on Accuracy

While BirdNET is highly accurate, it's not perfect. Factors like background noise, overlapping calls, and similar-sounding species can affect identification accuracy. Confidence scores help indicate how reliable each detection is. For scientific research or rare species confirmation, expert verification is recommended.
